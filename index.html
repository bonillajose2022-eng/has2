📚 Library Rules — CAN / CAN'T

🏛️ Welcome to the City Library!

The City Library is a wonderful place to read, study, and learn. But remember — there are rules!

✅ You CAN:

  • 📗 You can borrow up to 5 books.
  • 💻 You can use the computers for 1 hour.
  • 📝 You can take notes quietly.
  • 🎧 You can listen to audiobooks with headphones.
  • 📷 You can take photos of pages for study.

❌ You CAN'T:

  • 🍕 You can't eat food inside.
  • 📱 You can't make phone calls.
  • 🔊 You can't play loud music.
  • 🐕 You can't bring pets.
  • ✂️ You can't cut or damage books.

📌 Grammar Note

CAN = it is possible / allowed ✅

CAN'T = it is not possible / not allowed ❌

Structure: Subject + CAN/CAN'T + base verb

Example: "You can borrow books." | "You can't eat here."

🔗 Connectors — AND / BUT / OR

🔗 Connectors: AND, BUT, OR

➕ AND

Use AND to add information or join two similar ideas.

✅ I like cats and dogs.

✅ She reads books and watches films.

↔️ BUT

Use BUT to show a contrast or difference.

✅ I like swimming, but I don't like running.

✅ He is tall, but his brother is short.

❓ OR

Use OR to show a choice or alternative.

✅ Do you want tea or coffee?

✅ We can go to the park or the cinema.

📋 Have To / Has To

📋 Have To / Has To — Obligation

SubjectFormExample
I / You / We / Theyhave toI have to study.
He / She / Ithas toShe has to study.
I / You / We / They (❌)don't have toI don't have to cook.
He / She / It (❌)doesn't have toHe doesn't have to cook.

💡 Remember!

Use HAVE TO / HAS TO when something is necessary or obligatory.

❗ With he/she/it: HAS to (not HAVE to)

Structure: Subject + have to/has to + base verb
